Gallery of Indian Heritage

Explore Unique Aspects of India

Rajasthani Puppet

These traditional puppets, called Kathputli, are used in folk performances to narrate stories, legends, and moral lessons. They are vibrant, handcrafted, and an essential part of Rajasthan’s cultural heritage.

Madhubani Painting

Madhubani art originates from Bihar and is known for its intricate geometric patterns, natural dyes, and depiction of mythology, nature, and rituals. It’s traditionally done on walls and floors, now also on paper and cloth.

Onam Festival

Onam is a harvest festival celebrated in Kerala with elaborate floral arrangements (Pookalam), traditional dances, boat races, and grand feasts known as Onasadya, symbolizing prosperity and cultural unity.

Pushkar Camel Fair

The Pushkar Fair in Rajasthan is one of the world’s largest camel fairs. It features livestock trading, folk performances, competitions, and vibrant celebrations attracting tourists and locals alike.

Street Market

Indian street markets are bustling hubs of colors, spices, textiles, and handicrafts. They reflect the local lifestyle, vibrant culture, and the everyday life of millions of people across the country.

Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is India’s national animal and can be spotted in national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. It symbolizes strength, power, and India’s commitment to wildlife conservation.

Peacock Display

The Indian peacock, the national bird, is renowned for its vibrant feathers and elegant courtship dance. It appears in folklore, art, and decorations throughout India.

Temple Carvings

South Indian temples are famous for intricate stone carvings depicting gods, mythological stories, and cultural motifs. They reflect centuries of devotion, architecture, and craftsmanship.
